3.'Alchemy' Plaster and copper on canvas

When I look at an existence in real space and time, I morph it within my head in order to understand it in a different way, bending its limitations as to what else it can exist as. Pieces of the human body and ethereal surroundings come to fruition of existing in different ways, through de-familiarisation and the element of the uncanny, objects being familiar and not. Through creation, I am able to transcend the image out of time and space, giving it an alternate existence. Within my artwork it is important to have a sense of liminality, a sense of awkwardness and unsureness, the abstract within the work representing my imagination and subconscious.
I employ a sense of alchemy by preserving organic sensations and contradicting the existence of the form, which would inevitably decay, by representing them through mediums that stand the test of time, such as copper, resin or oil.
The elimination of the context of space and proportion creates a sense of infinite, defying limitations, mimicking the vast openness of the ocean or dessert.
Nature for me is a necessity and an axiom.
