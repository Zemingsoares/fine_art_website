Jessica Couch

In an age where we are equipped with the apparatus to curate, manipulate, modify and polish our exteriors as well as our characteristics, striving for the notion of pre-empted perfection is not an unacquainted sensation within many. A dedication and obsession to create visually stimulating, romanticised adaptations of a scenario is practiced within crowds on social media, and in this instance is the driving force behind my own Photographic, creative practice. An inability to regurgitate the romanticised imagery, which formally exists conceptually, drives my creative processes. The desired outcomes cannot always be recreated or translated as an exact interpretation of the illusory and idealised image. Consequentially forming the personal mentality that the literal image never reaches a point of completion, resulting in continuous developments within the initial ideas and within the practice itself. This narcissistic element of creation both restricts and feeds the creative muscle, that continues to be defined by progression.
