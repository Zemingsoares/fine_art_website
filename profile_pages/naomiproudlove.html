Naomi Proudlove

My work is a meditation on time as a way of making sense of what it means to be in the modern world and how we engage with the everyday existence. The repeated rhythm of daily life is observed through indexical action and object: traces of the body in motion recorded in an open-ended project of critical research. By engaging in the routine everyday, my work explores the dissolving distinction between art and environment, between process and finished work, and between inside and outside. The mundane repetitive activity of the domestic home, dominated by time-based routine, is juxtaposed with alternative temporal rhythms of the natural world in an attempt to engage with the world and question and consider what happens when nothing happens?
