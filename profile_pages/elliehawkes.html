2.'Alpine' Inkjet photographic prints on poly-film paper
3.'Magnolia Vine' Inkjet photographic prints on poly-film paper

Ellie Hawkes' practice explores natural forms, documenting nature through photographic processes. Images which the artist works with have resilient references to habitual matter and can be perceived as physical samples of nature. 'Archives of disappearing landscapes' is a term used to define her photographs, in the sense that global warming is slowly causing these picturesque scenes to deteriorate. The relationship between the image and internal/external space in which it is displayed is also questioned, considering what connections the image has to the architecture it is placed within. The form becomes an integration of an already existing space.
