TEXT IN ITALICS I AM IN VERBAL FLIGHT.

Phantom body. Animal sound. A pip under tongue. Rupture. Hum. A split slipping. Soft Grandfather milk eyes. Yellowing blue. Pink in the center. Silence. Silence. A bird passes underfoot.

Something like this, but not this.

Peer closely into this vast cavity, this promising wound. Perhaps it is here where the Bear herself lies waiting as another fractured articulation, a hollow in the ground whose empty abandonment brings me closer to her than her presence ever could. She is fleeting, and speaking of her with a broken voice which cracks under a weight it is unable to sustain, a voice which wobbles and strains, squints at each edge. A wavering hand tries to catch the words. I hope she may be embodied in this voice, in the broken parts which lay strewn across the space as a mark of another failed attempt to reach her phantom image. Trying to wrap a tongue around a ‘once was’ that slips. An ecstatic fiction, a mark of the presence and absence of the figures that are gone.

Trying to give these ghosts a body like mine, a body I can hold.
