My work reflects heavily, almost obsessively, on events in my own life, both positive and negative. I enjoy telling these autobiographical stories through paintings and drawings, finding the process of creation a good way to add a sense of closure on each chapter of my life, whether it be my ongoing Obsessive Compulsive Disorder, my recovery from Anorexia Nervosa in my teenage years or heartbreaks and loves and hobbies. In my recent works, I have been channelling my adoration of classic-style old-school tattoos in order to portray these events marked upon me. I find that having real tattoos on my own body in real life is a way in which I can manipulate and control how I look, adorning myself with images I find beautiful, in turn finally considering parts of me as beautiful. The artists from which I gain the most inspiration include Frida Kahlo, Tamara de Lempicka, Egon Schiele, Lucian Freud, Dante Gabriel Rossetti and Helen Chadwick.
