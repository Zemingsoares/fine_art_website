3. Red Lilies, concrete, acrylic and raw pigments on canvas,123x98cm, 2017

Amaury's work is built around three notion : the form , the frame and the support. His compositions are concrete based and the stratographic application of raw pigments as bandages allows the audience to interpret the work freely. Sometimes as maps, textures or landscapes. Destructuring the classical easel painting, Amaury's work is reflecting a will to interrogate spaces and their architecture. The application of his works in-situ on wall and floor raises perhaps a desire to hide or reveal their circulation but definetly to inhabit them, continuously interrogating spaces and the function of a painting.
