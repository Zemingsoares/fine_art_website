Glo Orpilla

1.'maria makiling, mano po'
2.'bamboo and almusal'
3.'the cow and the farmer'

wheelchair bound,
but not surrendering to time's cruelty
your skin tells a hundred stories, each spot marked by the eastern sun.
scars and wounds from the days of your youth
your eyes, turning to grey
they watch the sun rise once more
your hair, resonates the colour of pure sampaguita
blooming from the damp ground.
you spend your days with no company but the buzz from the daytime tv.

twenty two thirty five
play the cylindro in the dark
amidst the candle light and rain
your favourite instrument is clasped in your hand
as your melody sings.
