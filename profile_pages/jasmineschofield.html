“Jasmine Amber Schofield is a South-East London based artist, whose practice focuses on domesticity and topophilia (A strong sense of place, from Greek topos meaning ‘place’ and -philia being ‘love of’) around the notion of ‘Home’. Her installations represent scenarios of her family house referencing the emotions attached to familiarity and the transition of leaving home. Using recognisable objects combined with unconventional materials to create functionless ‘furniture’, and concrete as a metaphor for the act of change, the audience is invited to contemplate their own memories surrounding the idea of home.”
