Nathaniel Faulkner


Pseudoscience is a term that currently circulates my practice, it is a tool that identifies unscientific methods and theories that make scientific claims. This arguably perpetuates an objective and empirical theory of science, one which can refute the ‘unscientific’ practices of other cultures. This relationship between myth and science is where I try and situate myself and the things I make, often drawing from myths and symbols of ancient and pre-history, most of which I encounter via antiquities and historical sites. These archetypal motifs of the historical are combined with current references and products of contemporary culture.
