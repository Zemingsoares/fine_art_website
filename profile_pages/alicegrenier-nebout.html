Alice Grenier-Nebout

She encoutered the artistic world at the age of 3, she spent most of her childhood with her grandmother at her ceramics studio or surrounded by a cinematic environment : her mother who`s an actress or her father working behind the camera.
Being in the atelier of ceramics she was first exposed to the discovery of vivid colours transforming inside ovens, to the touch of different organic materials, building childhood symbols as cakes, castles, little people. She soon started to build her own universe of characters and stories.
She contantly travelled between Canada, France and all around the world to follow each of her parents.
She realised the importance of painting when in Canada with her father, they worked together in a massive wooden structure, realising the emotional role of art in connecting people through paint.
When a teenager, against her mother`s will, she felt the urge to fill her room walls with what later would become her biggest obsession : imaginitive worlds of colour .
She followed this instict and continued her progress into a fine art school till the age of 19.
Receiving proposals of collaborations with designers and fashionists, she created a wide range of patterns and images that were used for all different kinds of purposes.

She then moved to London to study Fine Arts at Central Saint Martins and proposed to make an exhibittion in Paris, she chose to explore the subject of Exode isolated from society in her studio in Britanny.

In this first exhibition opening, she sold a total of 11 pieces.
