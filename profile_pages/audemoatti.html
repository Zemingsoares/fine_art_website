I stage nothing, invisibly, it's harder than you think. My performative work is a non-event, yet its structure is very precise, as I want to make sure you don't notice it until I am done performing.

My writing is the exact opposite. It is loaded with images, and noticeably staged. The silences count as much as the text. It is important to let a message settle in before moving on to the next one. Take it as a score for thoughts.

They both complement each other and often overlap, sometimes making the boundary between my performances and my writing very blurry.
