Tuli Litvak

Tuli is interested in exploring the fragility and fragmentation of identity in a cross-cultural, media driven world.

Merging sculpture, film and performance practices, Tuli assembles events where the subtleties of political and social power structures are brought into light, broken apart and then reassembled to form more just structural alternatives. Following Alain Badiou’s definition of an event as a point of rapture where the margins are centralised. This forms the baseline of her practice.

In 2017, Tuli performed at the Tate Exchange as part of a collaboration between the Tate & CSM and also exhibited in ‘Transitions’, a group exhibition at the Crypt Gallery in Euston. In 2016, she participated in ‘Walking Backwards’, a short exhibition at the Camden Arts Centre. Earlier that year, photographs of her performances were projected onto the Royal Festival Hall at the South Bank Centre, for the 2016 ‘Women of the World Festival’. In March of that year,Tuli also exhibited a sculpture at the ‘Do Disturb’ Festival at Palais de Tokyo in Paris. In 2013, she was the winner of a D&AD ‘Best of Year’ student award in collaboration with Dazed & Confused and won a competition in 2012 in collaboration with The Photographers Gallery, where her work was projected.
